1. User Entity

Attributes: user_id (PK), username, password, role (Patient/Doctor)

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with Patient (if the user is a Patient).

One-to-One relationship with Doctor (if the user is a Doctor).

2. Patient Entity

Attributes: patient_id (PK), name, contact_info, dob, etc.

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with User (for authentication purposes).

One-to-Many relationship with Appointment (A Patient can have multiple Appointments).

One-to-Many relationship with Consultation Record (A Patient can have multiple Consultation Records).

3. Doctor Entity

Attributes: doctor_id (PK), name, specialty, contact_info, etc.

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with User (for authentication purposes).

One-to-Many relationship with Appointment (A Doctor can have multiple Appointments).

One-to-Many relationship with Consultation Record (A Doctor can have multiple Consultation Records).

4. Appointment Entity

Attributes: appointment_id (PK), appointment_date, status (Scheduled, Completed, Cancelled), patient_id (FK), doctor_id (FK)

Relationships:

Many-to-One relationship with Patient (Each Appointment is booked by one Patient).

Many-to-One relationship with Doctor (Each Appointment is booked with one Doctor).

One-to-One relationship with Consultation Record (Each Appointment results in one Consultation Record).

5. Consultation Record Entity

Attributes: consultation_record_id (PK), appointment_id (FK), symptoms, diagnosis, treatment_plan, prescribed_medications, doctor_notes, follow_up_required

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with Appointment (Each Appointment results in one Consultation Record).

Many-to-One relationship with Patient (A Patient can have multiple Consultation Records).

Many-to-One relationship with Doctor (A Doctor can create multiple Consultation Records).

One-to-One relationship with Bill (Each Consultation Record generates one Bill).

6. Bill Entity

Attributes: bill_id (PK), consultation_record_id (FK), amount, status (Paid, Pending), date_issued

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with Consultation Record (A Bill is generated for each Consultation Record).

One-to-One relationship with Payment (Each Bill has one Payment).

7. Payment Entity

Attributes: payment_id (PK), bill_id (FK), amount_paid, payment_method (Credit Card, Online Transfer, etc.), payment_date

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with Bill (Each Payment is linked to one Bill).

8. Prescription Entity

Attributes: prescription_id (PK), consultation_record_id (FK), medication_name, dosage, duration, instructions

Relationships:

One-to-One relationship with Consultation Record (Each Consultation Record may include one or more Prescriptions).

Many-to-One relationship with Inventory (A Prescription may reference multiple medications in the inventory).

9. Inventory Entity

Attributes: inventory_id (PK), medication_name, stock_quantity, unit_price, supplier_info

Relationships:

Many-to-One relationship with Prescription (A Medication from the Inventory can be prescribed in many Prescriptions).
